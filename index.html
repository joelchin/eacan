About EACAN

A regional network for children's anaesthesia

The East Anglian Children's Anaesthesia Network (EACAN) connects consultant paediatric and children's anaesthetists working across the East of England NHS region, from the two tertiary paediatric centres in Cambridge and Norwich to the district general hospitals across the wider region.

Our aim is to facilitate collaboration, share best practice, support trainees, and develop consistent, high-quality anaesthetic care for children across all levels of the network — from complex tertiary cases to routine day-case surgery at district level.

We hold biannual regional meetings, maintain a network WhatsApp group for real-time clinical discussion, and serve as a forum for regional audit, governance, and education.

Tertiary Centres

Addenbrooke's Hospital, Cambridge (CUH) and Norfolk & Norwich University Hospital (NNUH) provide specialist and complex paediatric anaesthesia services for the region.

District General Hospitals

DGHs across East Anglia provide anaesthesia for day-case and planned paediatric surgery, supported by the network and national guidelines including BAPO and APAGBI.

Regional Guidelines & Governance

EACAN supports adoption of national guidelines including APAGBI fasting recommendations, BAPO day-case guidance, and PPOC network resources.

Sister Networks

EACAN sits alongside the East Anglian Obstetric Anaesthesia Group (EAOAG) and the East Anglian Anaesthesia network.

What we do

Supporting great care for children across the region

EACAN activities span education, governance, and clinical collaboration.

Regional Meetings

Biannual one-day scientific and clinical case presentation meetings at Newmarket Racecourse, open to all paediatric anaesthetists in the region. CPD recognised.

Clinical Network

A regional WhatsApp group enables real-time clinical discussion, peer support, and rapid sharing of guidance updates across all hospitals in the network.

Guidelines & Audit

Disseminating national guidelines from APAGBI, BAPO, RCoA and the PPOC network, and supporting regional clinical audit and quality improvement projects.

Trainee Support

Supporting paediatric anaesthesia trainees in the East of England deanery through educational bursaries, mentoring, and access to the regional network.

Patient Safety

Promoting safe paediatric anaesthesia practice across DGHs, including guidance on case selection, appropriate escalation pathways, and incident learning.

Resource Sharing

Curating and sharing useful links, local SOPs, and educational resources including OrphanAnesthesia, BJAed, and national network guidance.
